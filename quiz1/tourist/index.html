Islamic Center Samarinda

So, this mosque isn't just big—it's massive. Like, top 5 biggest mosques in Southeast Asia. Built right by the Mahakam River, it has seven towers and a giant dome. Fun fact: the main tower is 99 meters tall to represent the 99 names of Allah.

Wisata Pesut Mahakam

So this one's special—on the Mahakam River, you can actually spot Pesut Mahakam. They're kinda chubby, cute, and lowkey look like dolphins in loaf mode 🐬✨. These dolphins are endangered and you can only find them in a few places in the world—Mahakam is one of them. Locals even consider them a symbol of good luck.

Desa Budaya Pampang

This place is like the cultural runway of Samarinda. It's a Dayak village where you can catch traditional dances, music, and rituals that go way back. The locals dress in full Dayak attire—beads, feathers, and tattoos that could honestly put modern fashion week to shame. Fun fact: you'll often see elders with super long earlobes—that's a Dayak tradition, and it's considered a symbol of wisdom + status.

Teras Samarinda

So this one's not like “heritage” or “nature” spot, but more of a lifestyle hub. Basically, it's a riverside hangout spot in Samarinda—think cafés, resto vibes, and chill spaces overlooking the Mahakam River 🌅✨. Teras Samarinda is super popular with locals for night vibes. People go there to eat, hang, or just soft-launch their situationships on Instagram stories.
